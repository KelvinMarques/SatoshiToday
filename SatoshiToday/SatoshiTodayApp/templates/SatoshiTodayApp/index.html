What is Sats?

"Sats" is short for Satoshis, which are the smallest unit of Bitcoin, named after its creator, Satoshi Nakamoto. A single Bitcoin can be divided into 100 million satoshis, meaning 1 satoshi (or "sat") is equal to 0.00000001 BTC. Just like cents divide the real or the dollar, satoshis divide Bitcoin, making it possible to make transactions with very small fractions of the currency, which facilitates micropayments and accessibility for those who want to buy small amounts of Bitcoin.
